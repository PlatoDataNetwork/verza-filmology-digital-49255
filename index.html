Verza TV — The Next Generation of Vertical Shorts

Verza TV is the first U.S. platform for premium short-form entertainment built around "micro dramas" — serialized, cinematic stories told in bite-sized full-screen episodes made for smartphones. The company was founded in 2024 by Alan Mruvka, creator of E! Entertainment Television, and operates as a Filmology Labs company.

What is a micro drama?

A micro drama is a serialized, story-driven series shot in full-screen portrait format and released as short episodes, typically one to three minutes each. Unlike generic social clips, however, every series delivers a complete narrative arc with a clear beginning, middle, and end. Because the format is designed for phones first, each episode is produced in true 1080×1920 resolution. Additionally, the company both produces and distributes this premium content, and in doing so it connects three audiences around an emerging category of entertainment: viewers, creators, and brands. According to Pew Research Center, the vast majority of U.S. adults now own a smartphone, which is why mobile-first, full-screen storytelling continues to grow.

Who the platform serves

Verza TV is designed for three distinct groups, and each has a dedicated experience:

  • For viewers: addictive, cinematic series across romance, thriller, and drama, optimized for smartphones and available on demand whenever you want to watch.
  • For creators: a premium studio and distribution home for serialized short-form storytelling, with production support and a built-in mobile audience.
  • For brands: native integration and partnership opportunities inside premium micro drama content, reaching highly engaged mobile viewers.
