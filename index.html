Verza TV — The Next Generation of Vertical Shorts

In short: Verza TV is the first U.S. platform for premium short-form vertical entertainment — serialized "micro dramas" that tell complete, cinematic stories in bite-sized vertical episodes made for smartphones. It was founded by Alan Mruvka, creator of E! Entertainment Television, and is a Filmology Labs company.

"We are building a premium home for vertical storytelling — cinematic micro dramas made for the way people actually watch today." — Alan Mruvka, Founder

What is a micro drama?

A micro drama is a serialized, story-driven series shot in a full-screen vertical format and released as short episodes. Unlike generic social clips, each series delivers a complete narrative arc. The company produces and distributes this premium content, connecting three audiences around a new category of entertainment: viewers, creators, and brands. According to Pew Research Center, the vast majority of U.S. adults now own a smartphone, and short-form vertical video makes up a growing share of mobile watch time.

Who the platform serves

  • For viewers: addictive, cinematic vertical series across romance, thriller, and drama, optimized for smartphones.
  • For creators: a premium studio and distribution home for serialized vertical storytelling.
  • For brands: native integration and partnership opportunities inside premium micro drama content.

By the numbers

  • Founded by the creator of E! Entertainment Television, a network that reached over 90 million U.S. households.
  • Short-form vertical video makes up more than 60% of mobile viewing time.
  • Episodes are produced in true vertical 1080×1920 format for full-screen mobile playback.
